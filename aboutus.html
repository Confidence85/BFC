About Us

Our Team

Oluwatomi Ogunleye and Confidence Nawali are two dynamic sophomore students at the esteemed University of Notre Dame. With a shared commitment to academic excellence and a passion for innovation. Oluwatomi is studying Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Energy Studies. Meanwhile, Confidence is studying computer science within the College of Engineering. The two leveraged the opportunity presented by the Boeing Flight Competition at NSBE’s 50th National Convention, to seamlessly fuse their expertise to showcase their passions. Their collaboration not only demonstrated their technical prowess but also underscored their ability to work harmoniously as a team, embodying the spirit of collaboration and excellence that defines their journey at Notre Dame.

Confidence Nawali

Confidence Nawali is a second year student at the University of Notre Dame studying Computer Science. Enamored by machine learning in the field of Computer Science, one of her long term goals is to hopefully secure a role in this discipline. Her most relevant experience is with the National Science Foundation’s REU program where she worked with a professor from her school on their Drone Research project. During this time, she was able to navigate complex technological landscapes and design applications that address real life needs. Confidence is keen on leveraging her background in computer science to work with diverse perspectives to foster innovation.

Oluwatomi Ogunleye

Originally from Maryland, Nigerian-American Oluwatomi Ogunleye is a Mechanical Engineering Major with a concentration in Aerospace Engineering and is expected to graduate in May 2026. Professionally, she is interested in AutoCAD, SolidWorks, and Blender in 3-D modeling. She also enjoys undergraduate research within Notre Dame's Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering department. Her love for the aerospace industry came from her childhood when she used to visit the NASA Goddard Space Flight Visitor Center several times a year as a child, and she hopes to help other black women in STEM see that the sky is the beginning, never the limit.
